Before You Begin

How to Use This Plan

🪑

Choose Your Chair

Use a sturdy, armless chair with a firm seat. Feet should rest flat on the floor. No wheels or rocking chairs. Keep it away from walls so arms can extend fully.

🌬

Breathe Slowly

In Tai Chi, breath drives movement. Inhale as you open or lift; exhale as you close or lower. Never hold your breath. If you feel dizzy, pause and breathe normally.

🐢

Move Like Water

All movements should be slow, continuous, and deliberate. There is no hurrying. Quality of movement matters far more than speed or repetitions.

📅

Rest Days Matter

Rest days (marked 休 below) are part of the program, not gaps. Use them to notice how your body feels. Light walking is welcome; vigorous exercise is not.

⏱

Duration

Week 1–2 sessions run ~15 minutes. Week 3–4 sessions run ~20–25 minutes. Start your timer only after you're seated and composed.

✅

Track Progress

Check the small circle on each day card after completing it. Use the progress journal at the end of this document to note how you feel each week.

Consult your physician before beginning this or any new exercise program, especially if you have cardiovascular, balance, or joint conditions. Stop and rest if you feel pain, dizziness, or shortness of breath.

Exercise Reference

The Movements

Each exercise below is used in the 28-day calendar. Refer back here anytime you need a description. All are performed seated unless noted.

Warm-Up

Seated March

Lift alternating knees slowly, as if walking in place. Keep spine tall. Arms may swing gently opposite to legs. 20–30 sec.

Warm-Up

Neck Rolls

Drop chin to chest, slowly roll right ear toward right shoulder, back to center, left ear toward left shoulder. Never roll fully backward. 30 sec.

Warm-Up

Arm Circles

Extend arms at shoulder height, draw slow circles forward 10 times, then backward 10 times. Breathe continuously.

Warm-Up

Ankle Rolls

Lift one foot, rotate the ankle 5 times clockwise, 5 counter-clockwise. Repeat other side. Grounds awareness in the feet.

Core / Flow

Cloud Hands

With soft elbows, sweep both hands slowly from left to right as if moving clouds across the sky. Torso rotates gently. 30–40 sec.

Core / Flow

Ward Off (Peng)

Hold arms in a rounded shape as if embracing a large ball. Slowly push outward with one forearm while the opposite hand curves below. 30 sec each side.

Core / Flow

Parting the Wild Horse's Mane

One hand rises to shoulder height (palm down), opposite hand descends to hip (palm up), as if parting a horse's mane. Alternate sides smoothly. 30 sec.

Core / Flow

Brush Knee

One hand sweeps low past the opposite knee while the other hand pushes forward at chest height. Alternate sides with flowing continuity. 30 sec.

Core / Flow

Repulse the Monkey

Draw one hand back to the ear while the opposite hand reaches forward, palm out, as if gently pushing away a monkey. Step back in the mind while seated. 30 sec.

Core / Flow

Wave Hands in Clouds

Like Cloud Hands but with more torso rotation and a gentle figure-8 path through center. One hand always moves opposite the other. 40 sec.

Strength

Seated Leg Lifts

Slowly straighten one knee until the leg is parallel to the floor, hold 3 sec, lower. Repeat other side. Strengthens quads and hip flexors. 10 reps each.

Strength

Seated Marching (Weighted)

Like Seated March but hold the knee at height for 2 seconds before lowering. Increases core engagement. 15–20 sec per leg.

Strength

Shoulder Shrugs

Inhale and draw shoulders up to ears; exhale and let them drop completely. Feel the contrast between tension and release. 10 reps.

Strength

Seated Chest Opener

Interlace fingers behind the head (or clasp hands behind the back), open elbows wide, inhale and lift the chest. Hold 3 sec, release. 8 reps.

Strength

Goddess Squat (Seated)

Sit at the edge of chair, feet wide, slowly rise to a partial stand (hover), then lower back down with control. 10–12 reps. Progress to full stand by Week 3.

Strength

Seated Row

Arms extended forward, draw both elbows back squeezing shoulder blades together as if rowing a boat. Hold 2 sec, return slowly. 10 reps.

Balance

Single Leg Hold

With hands on thighs for light support, lift one foot 2–3 inches off floor and hold still for 10 sec. Breathe. Repeat other side. Builds hip stability.

Balance

Toe Taps

Alternately tap each toe forward, then out to the side, then back to center. Slow and controlled. Improves ankle proprioception. 20 sec each side.

Stretch

Forward Fold

Seated at edge, slowly hinge at the hips and let the torso drape toward thighs (or as far as is comfortable). Breathe deeply. Hold 30–40 sec.

Stretch

Seated Cat-Cow

Hands on knees. Inhale: arch the back, lift chest (cow). Exhale: round the spine, drop chin (cat). Flow between both for 30–40 sec.

Stretch

Eagle Arms Stretch

Cross one arm under the other at the elbows, wrap forearms, lift elbows to shoulder height. Feel the stretch across upper back. 20 sec each side.

Stretch

Seated Twist

Hand on opposite knee, other hand behind you on the chair seat. Inhale to grow tall, exhale to rotate. Look over the shoulder. 20–30 sec each side.

Stretch

Hip Opener

Cross ankle over opposite knee (figure-4 position). Sit tall and gently lean forward from the hips until you feel a stretch in the outer hip/glute. 30–40 sec each side.

Closing

Gathering Qi

Arms wide at sides, slowly sweep hands inward to center at the belly (the dantian), as if gathering energy. Repeat 5–8 times. Traditional Tai Chi closing.

Closing

Seated Savasana

Rest both hands in lap, close eyes, breathe naturally for 1–2 minutes. Notice any stillness or warmth in the body. The most important move.

Advanced Wk3–4

Warrior I Pose (Seated)

Sit sideways on edge of chair, rear leg extended straight back (foot on floor), front knee at 90°. Arms sweep overhead. Hold 20–30 sec each side.

Advanced Wk3–4

Pigeon Pose (Seated)

Cross ankle over opposite knee. Flex the foot to protect the knee. Sit upright or gently lean forward. Far deeper hip stretch than basic hip opener. 30–40 sec.

Advanced Wk3–4

Hamstring Stretch (Seated)

Extend one leg out straight, flex the foot, and gently reach toward the toes. Keep the spine long. Do not round the back. 30 sec each side.
