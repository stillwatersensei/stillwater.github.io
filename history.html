History of Tai Chi and Qigong

Tai Chi and Qigong are traditional Chinese movement practices that combine posture, breath, focus, and slow intentional motion.

Tai Chi

Tai Chi developed as an internal martial art and later became widely practiced for balance, relaxation, coordination, and mindful movement. Modern Tai Chi is often taught as a gentle sequence of flowing forms.

Qigong

Qigong is an older umbrella term for breath, posture, and energy-cultivation practices. It can include seated, standing, moving, or meditative exercises.

How Stillwater uses them

Stillwater is not a replacement for in-person instruction. It is a calm, seated introduction inspired by Tai Chi and Qigong principles: breathe gently, move softly, and return to stillness.
